Club History

Founded on 6 March 1902 as Madrid Football Club, the club has traditionally worn a white home kit since inception. The honorific title real is Spanish for "royal" and was bestowed to the club by King Alfonso XIII in 1920 together with the royal crown in the emblem.

Real Madrid has played its home matches in the 81,044-capacity Santiago Bernabéu Stadium in downtown Madrid since 1947. Unlike most European sporting entities, Real Madrid's members (socios) have owned and operated the club throughout its history.

The club was estimated to be worth $5.1 billion in 2022, the world's most valuable football club. In 2023, it was the second highest-earning football club in the world, with an annual revenue of €714 million.

Club Milestones

1902
Club Foundation

Real Madrid was founded as Madrid Football Club by a group of football enthusiasts led by Julian Palacios.

1920
Royal Title

King Alfonso XIII granted the club his royal patronage, thus becoming Real Madrid.

1947
Santiago Bernabéu Stadium

The club moved to the newly built Estadio Santiago Bernabéu.

1955
European Cup Creation

Real Madrid was a key club in the creation of the European Cup, competition they would dominate.

2000
FIFA Club of the Century

Real Madrid was named FIFA Club of the 20th Century, cementing its legacy as the world's greatest club.

2022
14th Champions League Title

Real Madrid won their 14th UEFA Champions League, further solidifying their dominance in European football.

Santiago Bernabéu Stadium

The Home of Real Madrid

The Santiago Bernabéu Stadium is the home stadium of Real Madrid since its completion in 1947. It is located in the Chamartín district of Madrid, Spain.

With a current capacity of 81,044, it is the second-largest stadium in Spain and one of the world's most famous football venues.

Stadium Facts:
  • Capacity: 81,044 spectators
  • Opened: 14 December 1947
  • Record Attendance: 129,690 (vs AC Milan, 1956)
  • Pitch Size: 105m x 68m
  • Renovation: Ongoing modernization project

Manager

Xabi Alonso

Head Coach Since 2024
